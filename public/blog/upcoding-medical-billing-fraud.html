Medical Upcoding: How Hospitals Overcharge You (and How to Catch It)

Published 2026-02-18 · 7 min read · By BillFight AI Team

Upcoding is one of the most common — and most expensive — forms of medical billing fraud. It occurs when a healthcare provider bills for a more expensive service or procedure than what was actually performed. The National Health Care Anti-Fraud Association estimates healthcare fraud costs $68 billion annually, and upcoding is a major contributor.

How Upcoding Works

Every medical service has a CPT code with a corresponding price. Higher-level codes cost more. Upcoding means assigning a higher (more expensive) code than what the service warranted. The most common type is ER visit upcoding — being charged for a Level 4 or 5 visit when your condition only warranted a Level 2 or 3.

How to Spot Upcoding

Request your itemized bill and medical records. Compare the CPT codes on the bill to what's documented in your records. Look up the code descriptions online. If the description doesn't match your experience, the code may be wrong.

Common Upcoding Scenarios

  • ER visit coded at Level 5 for a non-life-threatening condition
  • Office visit coded as "comprehensive" when it was a brief consultation
  • Procedure coded as more complex than what was performed
  • New patient visit code used for an established patient

Key Takeaway

Upcoding is billing fraud, and you have every right to dispute it. If the codes on your bill don't match the services you received, submit a written dispute with evidence from your medical records.
