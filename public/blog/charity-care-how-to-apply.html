Hospital Charity Care: How to Apply and Get Your Bill Reduced

Published 2026-03-07 · 8 min read · By BillFight AI Team

There's a little-known program that could wipe out your hospital bill entirely: charity care. Most nonprofit hospitals are legally required to offer financial assistance to patients who can't afford their bills, and the income thresholds are more generous than you might think.

Did You Know? Nonprofit hospitals received $28 billion in tax breaks in 2020 alone. In exchange, they're required to provide charity care — but most don't actively tell patients about it.

What Is Charity Care?

Charity care (also called financial assistance) is a program offered by hospitals to reduce or eliminate bills for patients who can't afford to pay. Under IRS Section 501(r), all tax-exempt (nonprofit) hospitals must have a written financial assistance policy, make the policy widely available to patients, and not use extraordinary collection actions before determining eligibility.

Who Qualifies?

Eligibility varies by hospital, but most programs use the Federal Poverty Level (FPL) as a benchmark. Common thresholds include free care for patients at or below 200% FPL, reduced care (sliding scale) up to 300-400% FPL. For reference, 200% FPL in 2026 is approximately $31,200 for an individual or $64,400 for a family of four. Some hospitals set thresholds even higher, especially in high cost-of-living areas.

How to Apply

Step 1: Find the Hospital's Financial Assistance Policy

Every nonprofit hospital must publish this. Look on their website (search for "financial assistance" or "charity care"), ask the billing department, or check with the patient advocate.

Step 2: Get the Application

Request the financial assistance application form. Many hospitals have it available online or will mail it to you.

Step 3: Gather Documentation

You'll typically need recent tax returns or W-2s, pay stubs for the last 2-3 months, bank statements, documentation of other debts or hardship, and proof of household size.

Step 4: Submit and Follow Up

Submit your completed application with all supporting documents. Follow up weekly. Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks.

What If You Have Insurance?

Having insurance does not disqualify you from charity care. If your insurance left you with a large balance after paying their portion, you can still apply for financial assistance on the remaining patient responsibility amount.

What If You're Denied?

If your charity care application is denied, ask for the specific reason, appeal the decision in writing, check if you qualify for a reduced rate (sliding scale) even if not for full charity care, and contact your state's Attorney General or hospital licensing board if you believe the hospital isn't complying with 501(r) requirements.

Key Takeaway

Before paying a large hospital bill, always ask about financial assistance. The application takes about 30 minutes and could save you thousands — or eliminate the bill entirely. You have nothing to lose by applying.
