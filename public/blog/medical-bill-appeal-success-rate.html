Medical Bill Appeal Success Rates: What the Data Shows

Published 2026-02-27 · 6 min read · By BillFight AI Team

The most common reason people don't dispute medical bills is because they think it won't work. The data tells a very different story.

The Numbers

Research from multiple sources paints a clear picture: over 50% of medical bill disputes result in some form of reduction. For insurance claim denials specifically, the success rate on appeal ranges from 40-60% for internal appeals and up to 70% for external reviews.

What Increases Your Odds

Written appeals are far more effective than phone calls. Appeals that cite specific billing errors have the highest success rate. Including fair market pricing data strengthens your case significantly. Referencing applicable laws (No Surprises Act, state protections) signals that you're prepared to escalate. Having supporting documentation (medical records, EOBs) makes your case harder to dismiss.

What Decreases Your Odds

Vague complaints without specific charges identified, emotional language instead of factual arguments, not including supporting documentation, and missing appeal deadlines all reduce your chances.

Key Finding: Patients who submit written disputes with specific evidence are 3-5x more likely to receive a billing adjustment than those who simply call to complain.

Key Takeaway

The odds are in your favor. More than half of people who dispute medical bills get a reduction. The key is to do it in writing, be specific, include evidence, and cite applicable laws.
