Still Got a Big Bill After Insurance? Here's Why and What to Do

Published 2026-02-24 · 7 min read · By BillFight AI Team

You have health insurance, you went to an in-network provider, you did everything right — and you still got a bill for thousands of dollars. What happened?

Why Insurance Doesn't Cover Everything

Your deductible is the amount you pay before insurance kicks in, which can be $1,000-$8,000+ for high-deductible plans. After the deductible, you typically pay coinsurance (10-40% of the allowed amount). And certain services may require a copay regardless of your deductible status.

Check Your Out-of-Pocket Maximum

Every ACA-compliant plan has an out-of-pocket maximum — the most you can pay in a plan year. For 2026, the maximum is $9,200 for individual coverage. If you've already spent more than this, your insurance should cover 100% of additional in-network costs.

Common Issues to Check

  • Was the claim processed correctly? Check your EOB.
  • Were all providers actually in-network?
  • Was the service coded correctly?
  • Has your deductible already been met?
  • Are you approaching your out-of-pocket maximum?

Key Takeaway

A high bill after insurance doesn't always mean you owe it. Verify the claim was processed correctly, check your deductible and out-of-pocket maximum, and dispute any errors before paying.
