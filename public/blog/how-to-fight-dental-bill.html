How to Fight a Dental Bill: Dispute Strategies That Work

Published 2026-02-23 · 7 min read · By BillFight AI Team

Dental bills can be just as shocking as medical bills, especially for procedures like crowns, root canals, implants, and orthodontics. And they're just as disputable.

Common Dental Billing Issues

Upcoding (billing a complex procedure when a simple one was performed), unbundling (splitting one procedure into multiple charges), billing for services not rendered, and charging for "upgraded" materials without prior consent are all common in dental billing.

Dental Insurance Appeals

Dental insurance denials are appealed the same way as medical insurance denials. You have the right to an internal appeal and, depending on your state, an external review. Get your dentist to write a letter of medical necessity explaining why the procedure was needed.

Negotiation Tips

Many dental offices offer cash pay discounts of 10-30%. Ask about payment plans. Get a second opinion on recommended treatments. Compare prices at dental schools, which often charge 50-70% less for supervised procedures.

Key Takeaway

Dental bills follow the same dispute principles as medical bills: request an itemized statement, check for errors, compare pricing, and submit a written dispute for anything that seems wrong.
