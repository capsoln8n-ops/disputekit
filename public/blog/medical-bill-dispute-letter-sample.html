Medical Bill Dispute Letter: Free Sample You Can Use Today

Published 2026-02-17 · 6 min read · By BillFight AI Team

A well-crafted dispute letter is the most effective tool for challenging a medical bill. Below is a sample letter you can adapt to your specific situation.

Sample Dispute Letter Structure

Opening

State your name, account number, and that you are formally disputing specific charges on your bill dated [date]. This establishes the letter as a formal dispute rather than a casual complaint.

Identification of Disputed Charges

List each charge you're disputing with the date of service, CPT code, description, and billed amount. Be as specific as possible.

Basis for Dispute

For each charge, explain why it's wrong. Common bases include: the charge exceeds fair market rates, the service was billed incorrectly, the charge is a duplicate, the service was not rendered, or the charge violates the No Surprises Act.

Legal References

Cite applicable laws such as the No Surprises Act, your state's balance billing protections, the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, and state consumer protection statutes.

Resolution Request

State what you want: a corrected bill, a specific reduction amount, or a full write-off. Set a 30-day deadline for response.

Closing

Note that you're sending via certified mail and that the account should not be sent to collections while the dispute is being investigated.

Pro Tip

While this template provides a solid framework, the most effective dispute letters are customized with your specific charges, state laws, and fair market pricing data. Generic letters get generic responses. The more specific your letter is to your unique situation, the better your chances of success.
